QuantCode-Bench: A Benchmark for Evaluating the Ability of Large Language Models to Generate Executable Algorithmic Trading Strategies

Alexey Khoroshilov, Alexey Chernysh, Orkhan Ekhtibarov, Nini Kamkia, Dmitry Zmitrovich

Lime

Paper Code Data Leaderboard

QuantCode-Bench evaluates modern LLMs on their ability to generate executable algorithmic trading strategies for the Backtrader framework from natural-language descriptions. The benchmark contains 400 tasks of varying difficulty collected from Reddit, TradingView, StackExchange, GitHub, and synthetic sources. Models are evaluated through a four-stage pipeline in both single-turn and agentic multi-turn settings.

Evaluation Pipeline

A strategy is counted as successful only if it passes all four stages sequentially.

1

Compilation

Code is syntactically correct and can be interpreted without errors.

→
2

Backtest

Strategy executes on historical market data spanning diverse assets and timeframes without runtime errors.

→
3

Trade

Strategy places at least one trade on historical data.

→
4

Judge

LLM judge confirms semantic alignment with the task description.

Key Findings

1

Syntax is Solved

Frontier models achieve near-perfect compilation rates. The challenge has shifted to operational formalization of trading logic.

2

Agentic Feedback Helps

Best models jump from ~70-76% to 95-98% Judge Pass with iterative feedback, showing most errors are locally repairable.

3

Specification Quality Matters

Vague descriptions are harder for models than formal, parameterized ones, even when the latter are conceptually more complex.

4

General > Specialized

General-purpose models with strong instruction-following often outperform code-specialized models on domain-specific strategy generation.

Example Tasks

Representative examples from each difficulty level.

Source: StackExchange

Calendar-based monthly strategy for SPY on daily data over a five-year backtest window.

Indicator setup:

  • No technical indicators are used.
  • Signals are triggered only by the calendar event marking the first trading day of each month.

Entry rule:

  • Open a long position on the first trading day of each month, regardless of price action.

Exit rule:

  • Close all open positions on the last trading day of the month.

Risk management:

  • Position size is 100% of available capital with a fixed initial capital of $1000.
  • The strategy reports the initial and final portfolio value after backtesting.

Source: Reddit

SOFI mean reversion with intraday pullback or flag-pattern entries on the 15-minute timeframe.

Indicator setup:

  • Use EMA(10) and EMA(20) as short- and medium-term trend filters.
  • Use a volatility-sensitive filter based on VRVP.
  • Restrict entries to cases where price trades within 1% of the 52-week high.

Entry rules:

  • Enter long on a pullback to a gap-fill level or to a rising intraday EMA, followed by an upward reversal.
  • Alternatively, skip the initial move and enter on a breakout from a 15-minute flag pattern in the direction of the main trend.

Exit rules:

  • Close the position at a local stop-loss level or with a default stop of roughly 1–2% from entry.
  • Take profit at the 52-week high or at a default target of roughly 3–5%.
  • Exit if price closes the day below the 10- or 20-day EMA.

Risk management and filters:

  • Size the trade so that risk per position remains within 1–2% of total capital.
  • Avoid chasing the initial gap move; prefer asymmetric entries after retracement or consolidation.

Source: GitHub

One-minute scalping strategy for SNAP using momentum, liquidity, and volatility filters.

Indicator construction:

  • Use Stochastic(5,3,3) as a proxy for buyer/seller pressure and CrossOver(%K, %D) as the trigger.
  • Use RSI(3) for micro-momentum confirmation.
  • Use SMA(volume, 20) as a liquidity filter and ATR(14) with a threshold of 0.03 as a volatility filter.

Entry logic:

  • Enter long when %K crosses above %D, %K > 80, RSI(3) > 55, current volume exceeds 1.2 × SMA(volume, 20), and ATR(14) ≥ 0.03.
  • Enter short under the mirrored conditions: downward crossover, %K < 20, RSI(3) < 45, sufficient volume, and the same ATR filter.

Exit logic:

  • Use a take-profit of $0.01 from entry and a stop-loss of $0.02.
  • Close on signal reversal or after a timeout of three bars.

Implementation constraints:

  • Limit position size to 100–500 shares, with 100 shares as the default.
  • Trade only during the main session, skip the first three minutes, and require sufficient rolling volume; an optional $25,000 balance filter may be applied for PDT compliance.
